# North Port Fire Rescue — Strategic Plan Content
## FY2026–FY2029

---

## Agency Overview

**Mission**
Provide exceptional public safety services in a safe, compassionate, and professional manner.

**Vision**
*(To be confirmed by command staff)*

**Values**
Trust · Family · Dedication · Compassion

---

## Strategic Pillars

The plan is organized around six mission-driven pillars. These pillars reflect strategic intent rather than organizational structure. Each pillar contains goals sequenced across fiscal years, with explicit dependency relationships identifying gates that must be completed before downstream goals can begin.

---

## Pillar 1 — Operational Readiness

**Purpose:** Maintain and advance NPFR's capacity to respond safely and effectively to all emergency incidents within the community, with a well-equipped, well-positioned, and well-prepared operational force.

---

### P1.G1 — Evaluate the current deployment model
**Year:** FY26
**Predecessor:** None
**Owner:** TBD
**Gates:** P1.G3, P1.G5

Evaluate NPFR's current deployment model against incident data from the prior 36 months to identify gaps in response capability, unit utilization, and geographic coverage.

**Tasks**
1. Gather incident data from the last 36 months and separate by incident type.
2. Review and rank incident types by frequency, severity, and resource demand.
3. Evaluate the current deployment model against identified incident patterns.
4. Identify gaps in coverage, staffing configuration, or unit positioning.
5. Produce a deployment evaluation report with findings and recommendations.

**Success Metric:** A completed deployment evaluation report reviewed and accepted by command staff, with gaps and recommendations documented.

---

### P1.G2 — Develop and implement a two-set PPE management program
**Year:** FY26
**Predecessor:** None
**Owner:** TBD

Develop and implement a sustainable two-set PPE management program that establishes reserve inventory standards, lifecycle replacement strategies, and long-term funding projections to support firefighter readiness and departmental growth.

**Tasks**
1. Conduct a comprehensive assessment of current PPE inventory including primary, secondary, reserve, and specialty sizing needs.
2. Develop minimum reserve PPE inventory standards to support recruit academies, new hires, damaged gear replacement, and emergency replacements.
3. Create a long-term PPE lifecycle replacement model accounting for the recent bulk purchase, projected retirements, and department growth.
4. Develop procedures within Honeywell First to monitor PPE assignment, serviceability, inspection compliance, and replacement forecasting.
5. Implement a sustainable PPE readiness program with annual review processes, reserve inventory targets, and replacement funding recommendations.

**Success Metric:** A documented PPE lifecycle replacement model with annual funding projections adopted and integrated into the budget process.

---

### P1.G3 — Develop a major incident response planning program
**Year:** FY27
**Predecessor:** P1.G1
**Owner:** TBD

Develop and implement a major incident response planning program covering all identified threat types within NPFR's response model, aligned to the deployment gaps identified in P1.G1.

**Tasks**
1. Identify major incident threat types from the deployment evaluation findings.
2. Review current response plans for alignment with identified threats and deployment model.
3. Develop a plan for each identified threat type including resource, staffing, and training requirements.
4. Plan and conduct a large-scale drill to test at least one major incident plan.
5. Establish a regular review and update cycle for all major incident plans.

**Success Metric:** Documented response plans for all identified major incident threat types, with at least one plan validated through a full-scale or tabletop exercise.

---

### P1.G4 — Enhance hazardous materials response capability
**Year:** FY27
**Predecessor:** None
**Owner:** TBD

Enhance hazardous materials response capability by expanding technician-level certification, developing a target hazard pre-plan library, and establishing a regular HOT training cadence led by department-certified FTOs.

**Tasks**
1. Increase the number of personnel certified to the Hazardous Materials Technician level to serve as future FTOs.
2. Create a target hazard list for the city and develop incident response pre-plans for each site.
3. Establish annual or bi-annual HOT training and tabletop exercises led by Hazmat-certified FTOs.

5. Review and update any relevant policies or protocols to reflect capability changes.

**Success Metric:** Increased number of Hazmat Technician-certified personnel, a completed target hazard pre-plan library, and documented annual training schedule.

---

### P1.G5 — Establish logistics resource hubs
**Year:** FY27
**Predecessor:** P1.G1
**Owner:** TBD
**Gates:** P1.G6

Establish three strategically located logistics resource hubs using locker technology to improve access to EMS supplies, reserve equipment, and operational resources, while reducing replenishment demands.

**Tasks**
1. Conduct a needs assessment and geographic analysis using call volume, coverage, and resource utilization data from the deployment evaluation to identify three hub stations.
2. Develop a logistics resource hub program defining inventory categories, stocking levels, replenishment procedures, and accountability measures.

5. Evaluate program effectiveness by measuring replenishment frequency, labor hours, inventory availability, and user feedback.

**Success Metric:** Three logistics resource hubs operational with documented SOGs, trained personnel, and a baseline effectiveness evaluation completed within the first year of operation.

**Year:** FY28

**Owner:** TBD

**Tasks**
